# Document 2: Competitive Landscape & Feasibility Analysis

## Document Structure

This document provides a thorough competitive and feasibility analysis. It should be 15-25 pages and rely heavily on web research data.

---

## Sections

### 1. Title Page
- Document title: "{Product Name} — Competitive Landscape & Feasibility Analysis"
- Subtitle: "Market Intelligence & Strategic Assessment"
- Date, version, confidentiality notice

### 2. Table of Contents
- Auto-generated TOC using Heading1/Heading2 styles

### 3. Executive Summary (1-2 pages)
- Key findings from competitive analysis
- Top 3-5 competitive threats and opportunities
- Feasibility verdict: Go / Conditional Go / No-Go recommendation
- Critical risks and top mitigation strategies
- Summary of competitive positioning

### 4. Industry Overview & Market Trends (2-3 pages)
- **Industry landscape**: Current state of the market
  - Market maturity stage (emerging, growth, mature, declining)
  - Key market drivers and tailwinds
  - Headwinds and challenges
- **Technology trends**: Relevant technology shifts affecting the market
  - AI/ML adoption, cloud migration, mobile-first, etc.
  - Emerging technologies that could disrupt
- **Regulatory environment**: Relevant regulations, compliance requirements
- **Market dynamics**: Consolidation trends, M&A activity, funding landscape
- **Format**: Use data from web research — cite specific reports, analysts, or news

### 5. Competitor Profiles & Deep Dives (3-5 pages)
- Profile 4-6 competitors (mix of direct, indirect, and emerging)
- **For each competitor**:

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Company | Name, founded, HQ, funding/revenue |
| Product | Core offering, key features |
| Target Market | Who they serve, market position |
| Pricing | Pricing model, tiers, price points |
| Strengths | What they do well |
| Weaknesses | Where they fall short |
| Recent Moves | Latest product launches, funding, partnerships |
| Threat Level | Low / Medium / High — with justification |

- **Data sources**: Pull from web research — company websites, review sites (G2, Capterra), news articles, Crunchbase
- Research queries to run:
  ```
  {competitor} pricing plans features
  {competitor} reviews G2 Capterra
  {competitor} funding revenue growth
  {competitor} product updates news {current_year}
  ```

### 6. Feature Comparison Matrix (1-2 pages)
- **Format**: Large comparison table across all analyzed competitors + our product

| Feature | Our Product | Competitor A | Competitor B | Competitor C |
|---------|-------------|-------------|-------------|-------------|
| Feature 1 | ✓ Full | ✓ Full | ~ Partial | ✗ None |
| Feature 2 | ✓ Full | ✗ None | ✓ Full | ~ Partial |
| Feature 3 | Planned | ✓ Full | ✓ Full | ✓ Full |
| ... | | | | |

- Use symbols: ✓ (full support), ~ (partial), ✗ (not available), "Planned" for roadmap items
- **Categories** to compare:
  - Core functionality
  - Integrations / API
  - Security / Compliance
  - Pricing / Value
  - Support / SLA
  - Mobile / Cross-platform
  - AI / Automation capabilities
  - Customization / Extensibility

| **Internal** | **Strengths** | **Weaknesses** |

- Each point should be specific and actionable, not generic
- Cross-reference with competitor data and market trends

### 9. Technical Feasibility Assessment (1-2 pages)
- **Technology stack assessment**: Can the required technology be built/integrated?
- **Build vs. buy decisions**: Key components and recommendations
- **Scalability analysis**: Can the architecture handle projected growth?
- **Integration requirements**: APIs, data sources, third-party services
- **Technical risks**: Complexity, dependencies, skill gaps
- **Timeline estimate**: Development milestones and resource needs
- **[PLACEHOLDER: Internal engineering team to validate technical assumptions]**

### 10. Operational Feasibility (1-2 pages)
- **Team requirements**: Roles needed, current gaps
- **Process readiness**: Support, onboarding, sales processes
- **Infrastructure**: Hosting, monitoring, security operations
- **Legal / Compliance**: Data privacy, terms of service, certifications needed
- **Customer support model**: Channels, SLAs, staffing
- **Operational risks** and mitigation

- **Revenue projections** (conservative, base, optimistic scenarios):

- **Cost structure**: Development, infrastructure, marketing, personnel
- **Funding requirements**: If applicable, how much and when
- **ROI analysis**: Expected return timeline
- **[PLACEHOLDER: Finance team to validate projections with internal data]**

### 12. Barriers to Entry & Moats (1 page)
- **Existing barriers**: What makes it hard for new entrants?
  - Technology complexity
  - Regulatory requirements
  - Capital requirements
  - Network effects
  - Brand / trust / relationships
- **Our moats**: What defensible advantages can we build?
  - Data moats
  - Technology moats
  - Network effects
  - Switching costs
  - Brand and community
- Rate each moat: Strength (Strong / Moderate / Weak) and timeline to build

### 13. Risks & Mitigation Strategies (1-2 pages)
- **Risk register** table:

| Risk | Category | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation Strategy |
|------|----------|-----------|--------|-------------------|
| Key competitor launches similar feature | Competitive | High | High | Accelerate differentiated features |
| Slower than expected adoption | Market | Medium | High | Expand channel partnerships |
| Technical scalability issues | Technical | Low | High | Load testing, architecture review |
| Regulatory changes | Regulatory | Low | Medium | Compliance monitoring, legal counsel |
| Key hire dependency | Operational | Medium | Medium | Cross-training, documentation |

- Prioritize by risk score (Likelihood × Impact)
- Include contingency plans for top 3 risks

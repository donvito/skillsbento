# Document 3: Product Roadmap & Partnership Strategy

## Document Structure

This document covers the product roadmap and partnership strategy. It should be 12-20 pages and translate competitive insights into actionable plans.

---

## Sections

### 1. Title Page
- Document title: "{Product Name} — Product Roadmap & Partnership Strategy"
- Subtitle: "Strategic Development Plan & Partnership Framework"
- Date, version, confidentiality notice

### 2. Table of Contents
- Auto-generated TOC using Heading1/Heading2 styles

### 3. Product Vision & Strategy (1-2 pages)
- **Vision statement**: Where the product is heading in 3-5 years (1-2 sentences)
- **Mission statement**: What the product does today and why it matters
- **Strategic pillars**: 3-5 key strategic themes that guide development
  - Example: "AI-First Automation", "Enterprise Security", "Developer Experience"
- **Success criteria**: What does success look like at 6mo, 12mo, 24mo?
- **Strategic principles**: Decision-making guidelines for prioritization
  - Example: "Customer pain > feature parity", "Platform over point solution"

### 4. Current State / MVP Overview (1-2 pages)
- **Current product status**: What exists today
- **Core capabilities**: Features that are live and working
- **Technical architecture overview**: High-level architecture description
  - Key technology choices and rationale
  - Infrastructure and deployment model
- **Known limitations**: Honest assessment of current gaps
- **Customer feedback themes**: What early users / beta testers are saying
- **[PLACEHOLDER: Insert current product screenshots or architecture diagram]**

### 5. Feature Prioritization (2-3 pages)
- **Framework**: Use MoSCoW or RICE — pick based on product stage

**MoSCoW Method** (better for early-stage / MVP):

| Priority | Features | Rationale |
|----------|----------|-----------|
| **Must Have** | Feature A, Feature B | Core to value prop, blocks adoption without |
| **Should Have** | Feature C, Feature D | Important but workarounds exist |
| **Could Have** | Feature E, Feature F | Nice-to-have, enhances experience |
| **Won't Have (now)** | Feature G, Feature H | Deferred — future consideration |

**RICE Scoring** (better for growth-stage with data):

| Feature | Reach | Impact | Confidence | Effort | RICE Score |
|---------|-------|--------|-----------|--------|------------|
| Feature A | 5000 | 3 | 90% | 2 | 6,750 |
| Feature B | 3000 | 2 | 80% | 1 | 4,800 |
| Feature C | 8000 | 1 | 70% | 3 | 1,867 |

- **Scoring explanations**: Explain the criteria and how scores were derived
- **Dependencies**: Note features that depend on others
- **Cross-reference**: Tie features back to competitive gaps from Document 2

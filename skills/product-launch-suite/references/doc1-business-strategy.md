# Document 1: Business & Market Strategy

## Document Structure

This document is the foundational strategy document. It should be 15-25 pages and cover the full go-to-market plan.

---

## Sections

### 1. Title Page
- Document title: "{Product Name} — Business & Market Strategy"
- Subtitle: "Go-to-Market Plan & Growth Strategy"
- Date, version, confidentiality notice

### 2. Table of Contents
- Auto-generated TOC using Heading1/Heading2 styles
- Include page numbers

### 3. Executive Summary (1-2 pages)
- **Purpose**: High-level overview of the entire strategy in 3-5 paragraphs
- **Content**:
  - What the product is and the problem it solves
  - Target market and size of opportunity
  - Business model and revenue potential
  - Key strategic pillars
  - Expected timeline to key milestones
- **Tone**: Compelling and concise — this should stand alone as a briefing

### 4. Market Sizing: TAM / SAM / SOM (1-2 pages)
- **TAM (Total Addressable Market)**: The entire revenue opportunity if 100% market share
  - Include source data and methodology (top-down or bottom-up)
  - Reference industry reports, analyst data from web research
- **SAM (Serviceable Addressable Market)**: The segment the product can realistically serve
  - Define geographic, demographic, or feature-based constraints
- **SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market)**: Realistic near-term capture (1-3 years)
  - Based on go-to-market capacity, competition, adoption rates
- **Format**: Include a visual table or nested breakdown:

| Market Layer | Definition | Estimated Size |
|-------------|------------|----------------|
| TAM | Total market for {category} | $X.XB |
| SAM | {geographic/segment} subset | $X.XB |
| SOM | Realistic Year 1-3 capture | $XXM |

### 5. Ideal Customer Profile & Buyer Personas (2-3 pages)
- **Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)**:
  - Company size (employees, revenue)
  - Industry verticals
  - Technology maturity / adoption stage
  - Budget range
  - Pain points that map to the product's value
- **Buyer Personas** (2-3 personas):
  - Name, title, demographics
  - Goals and motivations
  - Pain points and frustrations
  - Decision-making process
  - Information sources and channels
  - Objections and concerns
- **Format**: Use a structured table or card layout for each persona

### 6. Value Proposition (1 page)
- **Core value proposition statement**: One clear sentence
- **Value proposition canvas** or framework:
  - Customer Jobs (what they're trying to do)
  - Pains (obstacles, risks, frustrations)
  - Gains (desired outcomes, benefits)
  - Pain Relievers (how the product addresses pains)
  - Gain Creators (how the product delivers gains)
- **Differentiators**: 3-5 unique selling points vs. alternatives

### 7. Business Model & Revenue Streams (1-2 pages)
- **Business model type**: SaaS, marketplace, freemium, usage-based, etc.
- **Revenue streams**:
  - Primary revenue (subscriptions, transactions, etc.)
  - Secondary revenue (upsells, add-ons, services)
  - Future revenue opportunities
- **Unit economics** (where known or estimable):
  - Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)
  - Lifetime Value (LTV)
  - LTV:CAC ratio target
  - Payback period
- **Format**: Include a revenue model table

### 8. Pricing Strategy & Tiers (1-2 pages)
- **Pricing philosophy**: Value-based, competitive, penetration, freemium, etc.
- **Pricing tiers** (typically 3-4):

| Tier | Name | Price | Target | Key Features |
|------|------|-------|--------|-------------|
| Free/Starter | {name} | $0/mo | Individual users | Basic features |
| Professional | {name} | $XX/mo | SMBs | Core features + support |
| Enterprise | {name} | Custom | Large orgs | Full platform + SLAs |

- **Pricing justification**: Reference competitor pricing from research
- **Discounting strategy**: Annual vs. monthly, volume discounts
- **[PLACEHOLDER: Confirm final pricing with internal stakeholders]**

### 9. Go-to-Market Strategy (2-3 pages)
- **Launch phases**:
  - Phase 1: Private beta / early access (timeline, goals)
  - Phase 2: Public launch (timeline, channels, activities)
  - Phase 3: Scale & expand (timeline, growth levers)
- **GTM motions**:
  - Product-led growth (PLG) elements
  - Sales-led growth elements
  - Community-led growth elements
- **Launch activities**: Specific tactics for each phase
- **Timeline**: Include a phased timeline table

### 10. Distribution Channels (1 page)
- **Direct channels**: Website, app stores, direct sales
- **Indirect channels**: Partners, resellers, marketplaces
- **Content channels**: Blog, social, community
- **Channel prioritization**: Rank by expected impact and effort
- **Format**: Channel matrix table with reach, cost, priority

### 11. Marketing & Demand Generation Plan (2-3 pages)
- **Content marketing**: Blog, whitepapers, case studies, webinars
- **SEO / SEM strategy**: Target keywords, paid search plan
- **Social media**: Platform selection, content cadence, community building
- **Email marketing**: Nurture sequences, newsletter strategy
- **PR & thought leadership**: Press strategy, speaking opportunities
- **Paid acquisition**: Channels, budget allocation, expected CAC
- **Partnerships & co-marketing**: Joint activities with partners
- **Budget allocation**: High-level marketing budget breakdown table

### 12. Key Metrics & Success KPIs (1-2 pages)
- **North star metric**: The single most important metric
- **KPI framework** organized by category:

| Category | Metric | Target (6mo) | Target (12mo) |
|----------|--------|--------------|----------------|
| Acquisition | MQLs/month | X | Y |
| Activation | Trial-to-paid rate | X% | Y% |
| Revenue | MRR | $X | $Y |
| Retention | Monthly churn | X% | Y% |
| Efficiency | CAC:LTV ratio | X:1 | Y:1 |

- **Reporting cadence**: Weekly, monthly, quarterly review structure

## Formatting Notes

- Use professional Navy blue (#2E5090) for heading accents and table headers
- Include a confidentiality notice on the title page
- Add page numbers in the footer
- Use consistent spacing: 1.15 line height, 6pt after paragraphs
- Tables should have shaded headers and light alternating rows
